Scalability is the term used to describe a system's ability to cope with increased load. 

Scalability is not a one-dimensional label - it is meaningless to say 'X is scalable' or 'Y does not scale' 

Discussing scalability means considering questions like: 
* If the system grows in a particular way, what are our options for coping with the growth?
* How can we add computing resources to handle the additional load?
* Based on current growth projections, when will we hit the limits of our current architecture?

If your application is handling a growing amount of load, you will learn where performance bottlenecks lie and along which dimensions you need to scale. At that point, it is time to start worrying about techniques for scalability
